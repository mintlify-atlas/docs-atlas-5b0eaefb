## General Questions

<Accordion title="Do QR codes created with QRcodeer expire?">
  No, QR codes generated with QRcodeer never expire. Once you create and download a QR code, it will work indefinitely. The QR code contains the data directly embedded in it, so there's no reliance on external services or servers.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Do I need to create an account to use QRcodeer?">
  No account required! QRcodeer is completely free to use without any signup. Simply visit the app, enter your URL or text, customize your QR code, and download it immediately.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Is QRcodeer really free?">
  Yes, QRcodeer is 100% free with no hidden costs, subscriptions, or premium tiers. All features including custom logos, different sizes, and unlimited downloads are available to everyone at no charge.
</Accordion>

## QR Code Features

<Accordion title="What sizes are available for QR codes?">
  QRcodeer offers three size options:
  - **128px** - Good for digital use on websites or mobile apps
  - **256px** - Versatile size suitable for both digital and small print materials
  - **512px** - Best for printing on posters, flyers, or larger materials where quality matters
  
  All sizes are PNG format for crisp, clear quality.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I add a logo or image to my QR code?">
  Yes! QRcodeer supports adding a center logo or image to your QR code. This is a great way to brand your QR codes with your company logo or make them more visually appealing. The logo is automatically sized to maintain QR code scannability.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What file format are the downloaded QR codes?">
  All QR codes are downloaded as PNG files. PNG is a lossless format that ensures your QR codes remain sharp and scannable, whether you're using them digitally or printing them.
</Accordion>

## Technical Questions

<Accordion title="What browsers are supported?">
  QRcodeer works on all modern web browsers including:
  - Chrome, Edge, and other Chromium-based browsers
  - Firefox
  - Safari
  - Mobile browsers on iOS and Android
  
  No special plugins or extensions are required.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do I ensure my QR codes scan properly?">
  To ensure reliable scanning:
  - Test your QR code with multiple devices and QR code readers before widespread use
  - Maintain good contrast - the default black on white provides optimal scanning
  - Don't make the QR code too small when printing (at least 2cm x 2cm)
  - Ensure the printed or displayed QR code isn't distorted or stretched
  - Avoid adding logos that are too large (keep them under 20% of the QR code area)
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What's the best size for printing QR codes?">
  For printing, use the **512px** size option for best quality. When printing:
  - Minimum recommended print size: 2cm x 2cm (0.8" x 0.8")
  - Ideal print size: 3-4cm x 3-4cm (1.2"-1.6" x 1.2"-1.6")
  - For posters or billboards: scale up proportionally based on viewing distance
  
  The larger the print size, the easier it is to scan from a distance.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I use QRcodeer QR codes for commercial purposes?">
  Yes! QR codes generated with QRcodeer can be freely used for any purpose, including:
  - Business cards and marketing materials
  - Product packaging
  - Restaurant menus
  - Event tickets and promotions
  - Retail displays
  - Educational materials
  
  There are no usage restrictions or licensing fees.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Why are the QR codes black and white?">
  QR codes use black on white because this provides the highest contrast for reliable scanning across all devices and lighting conditions. While colored QR codes are possible, black and white ensures maximum compatibility and scan success rates.
</Accordion>
