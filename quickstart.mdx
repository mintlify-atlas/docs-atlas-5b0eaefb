Get started with QRcodeer and create your first QR code in just a few simple steps. This guide will walk you through the entire process from entering your content to downloading your customized QR code.

## Create your first QR code

<Steps>
  <Step title="Visit the app">
    Navigate to [https://qrcodeer.dev](https://qrcodeer.dev) in your web browser. The app works on desktop and mobile devices—no download or signup required.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter your content">
    In the **Content** field, enter the text or URL you want to encode. You can use:
    
    - URLs: `https://example.com`
    - Email addresses: `mailto:hello@example.com`
    - Plain text: `Hello, World!`
    - Phone numbers: `tel:+1234567890`
    - Any other text you want to encode
    
    <Info>
      The QR code preview updates in real-time as you type, so you can see exactly what your QR code will look like.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Customize size and appearance">
    Choose your preferred QR code size:
    
    - **128px** - Ideal for small digital displays or compact designs
    - **256px** - Balanced size for most use cases (default)
    - **512px** - High resolution for print materials or large displays
    
    **Optional: Add a center image**
    
    Click the **Upload image** button to add a logo or icon to the center of your QR code. This is perfect for branding:
    
    1. Click **Upload image**
    2. Select an image file from your device (PNG, JPG, or any image format)
    3. The image will appear in the center of your QR code at 20% of the total size
    4. Click **Remove** if you want to change or delete the center image
    
    <Note>
      When you add a center image, QRcodeer automatically increases the error correction level to "High" to ensure the QR code remains scannable even with the center portion covered.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Download your QR code">
    Once you're happy with the preview, click the **Download PNG** button. Your QR code will be saved as a high-quality PNG file named `qrcode.png`.
    
    The downloaded file includes:
    - Your QR code pattern
    - White background
    - Center image (if you added one)
    - Your selected size dimensions
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Example use cases

Here are some common ways to use QRcodeer:

**Link to your website**
```
https://yourwebsite.com
```
Add your logo as the center image for branded QR codes on business cards or marketing materials.

**Share Wi-Fi credentials**
```
WIFI:T:WPA;S:NetworkName;P:Password;;
```
Generate a QR code that automatically connects devices to your Wi-Fi network.

**Contact information**
```
BEGIN:VCARD
VERSION:3.0
FN:John Doe
TEL:+1234567890
EMAIL:john@example.com
END:VCARD
```
Create a scannable contact card that saves directly to phones.
