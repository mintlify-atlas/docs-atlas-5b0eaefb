## Choosing the Right Size

Selecting the appropriate QR code size depends on how you plan to use it:

### 128px - Digital Use
Best for:
- Embedding in websites
- Mobile app interfaces
- Email signatures
- Social media posts

<Note>
  128px works well on screens but may lose quality if printed larger than 3-4cm.
</Note>

### 256px - Versatile Option
Best for:
- Small print materials (business cards, flyers)
- Digital displays
- Documents and presentations
- Most general-purpose uses

### 512px - High Quality
Best for:
- Large print materials (posters, banners)
- Professional marketing materials
- Packaging design
- Any situation requiring maximum quality

<Tip>
  When in doubt, choose 512px. Larger files give you more flexibility and maintain quality when scaled.
</Tip>

## Adding Logos and Images

QRcodeer allows you to add a center logo to personalize your QR codes. Follow these guidelines:

### Logo Size Ratio
The optimal logo size is **no more than 20% of the total QR code area**. QRcodeer automatically handles this sizing to ensure your QR code remains scannable.

<Warning>
  Logos that are too large can interfere with scanning. QR codes have built-in error correction, but oversized logos may exceed this tolerance.
</Warning>

### Logo Design Tips
- Use simple logos with clear shapes
- Ensure good contrast between logo and QR code background
- Square or circular logos work best
- Avoid very detailed or intricate designs

### When to Skip the Logo
- Mission-critical applications (event tickets, payment codes)
- Very small print sizes
- Low-quality printing conditions
- When maximum scan reliability is essential

## Testing Your QR Codes

Always test QR codes before distributing them widely.

<Accordion title="Testing Checklist">
  - [ ] Test with at least 3 different devices (iOS, Android, different brands)
  - [ ] Test with both native camera apps and dedicated QR readers
  - [ ] Test in different lighting conditions
  - [ ] If printing, test with a physical print sample
  - [ ] Verify the destination URL is correct
  - [ ] Check that the landing page is mobile-friendly
</Accordion>

<Tip>
  Free QR code reader apps often have better scanning capabilities than native camera apps. Test with both types.
</Tip>

## Printing Guidelines

### Minimum Print Sizes
- **Absolute minimum**: 2cm x 2cm (0.8" x 0.8")
- **Recommended minimum**: 3cm x 3cm (1.2" x 1.2")
- **Ideal for posters**: 5cm x 5cm (2" x 2") or larger

### Print Quality Considerations
- Use **512px** size for all print projects
- Print on matte or non-glossy surfaces when possible (reduces glare)
- Ensure your printer settings are set to highest quality
- Avoid printing on textured or patterned backgrounds
- Leave adequate white space (quiet zone) around the QR code - at least 4-5mm

<Warning>
  Never stretch or distort QR codes when resizing. Always maintain the square aspect ratio or scanning will fail.
</Warning>

### Distance and Size Relationship
For viewing from a distance:
- **Arm's length (0.5m)**: 3cm minimum
- **Across a room (3m)**: 10cm minimum
- **Billboard (10m+)**: 30cm or larger

## Color and Contrast

QRcodeer generates black QR codes on white backgrounds for a good reason:

### Why Black and White?
- **Maximum contrast** ensures reliable scanning in all lighting
- **Universal compatibility** with all QR code readers
- **Consistent performance** across different devices
- **Professional appearance** suitable for any context

<Note>
  While some tools create colored QR codes, black on white provides the highest scan success rate - often 95%+ across all conditions.
</Note>

### Background Considerations
When placing your QR code:
- Always use on white or very light backgrounds
- Avoid placing on photos, patterns, or busy backgrounds
- Ensure clean borders (quiet zone) around all sides
- Don't place near other QR codes or barcodes

## URL Management

### Shorten Long URLs
For QR codes containing URLs:

<Tip>
  Shorter URLs create simpler QR codes with larger modules (dots), making them easier to scan. Consider using a URL shortener for long links.
</Tip>

Benefits of shorter URLs:
- Simpler QR code patterns
- More reliable scanning
- Smaller file sizes
- Better error correction tolerance

### URL Best Practices
- Use HTTPS for security
- Test the destination URL on mobile devices
- Ensure the landing page loads quickly
- Make sure the page is mobile-responsive
- Avoid URLs with special characters when possible

## Strategic Placement

### Marketing Materials
- **Business cards**: Bottom corner or back side
- **Flyers and posters**: Below main call-to-action
- **Brochures**: Near relevant content sections
- **Product packaging**: Flat, accessible surface

### Physical Locations
- **Eye level** is ideal for easy scanning
- **Well-lit areas** improve scan success
- **Stable surfaces** (avoid moving or hanging placement)
- **Accessible locations** where people can stop and scan

<Info>
  QR codes on moving objects (vehicles, rotating displays) are difficult to scan. Use them on stable, stationary surfaces.
</Info>

### Digital Placement
- Email newsletters (linked to landing page)
- Social media posts and profiles
- Website footers for quick mobile access
- Digital receipts and confirmations
- Presentation slides

## Common Use Cases

### Restaurant Menus
- Use **256px or 512px** for table tents
- Place on every table in a protective stand
- Include simple instructions ("Scan for menu")
- Ensure WiFi is available for customers

### Event Check-in
- Use **512px** for printed tickets
- Avoid logos on critical check-in codes
- Test with your check-in system beforehand
- Print on white cardstock for best results

### Product Information
- Link to manuals, setup guides, or support pages
- Place on product packaging near opening
- Use **256px or 512px** depending on package size
- Consider adding "Scan for instructions" text

### Marketing Campaigns
- Track different campaigns with unique URLs
- Include QR codes on all print advertising
- Place prominently with clear call-to-action
- Link to mobile-optimized landing pages

<Tip>
  Add a short text prompt near your QR code like "Scan to learn more" or "Scan for special offer" to increase engagement by 30-40%.
</Tip>

## Accessibility Considerations

- Always include a text URL or alternative access method
- Don't rely solely on QR codes for critical information
- Consider users who may not have smartphones
- Ensure the linked content is accessible (screen reader friendly)
- Use descriptive text near the QR code explaining what it links to

## Troubleshooting Scan Issues

If your QR code isn't scanning reliably:

1. **Check the URL** - Verify it's correct and active
2. **Increase size** - Try a larger print or display size
3. **Improve contrast** - Ensure clean white background
4. **Remove or reduce logo** - Test without center image
5. **Check quiet zone** - Add more white space around edges
6. **Test lighting** - Ensure adequate, even lighting
7. **Verify print quality** - Check for smudging or distortion

<Warning>
  If you've laminated or covered your QR code with glossy material, glare may prevent scanning. Use matte finishes when possible.
</Warning>
